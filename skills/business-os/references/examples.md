# Business OS — Shape Patterns (Fallback Reference)

> **Important**: These are FALLBACK patterns to use only when you have no user-specific data to draw from. Always prefer examples generated from the user's own connectors, files, meeting notes, and conversations. The goal of the skill is to produce content from REAL data — these patterns just show shape variety so you know what good output looks like across business types.

Three shape patterns here: solo consultant, small agency, local service business. Use them for inspiration on structure and density — never as a template to copy verbatim. All names, companies, and data are fictional.

## Pattern 1: Solo Consultant (Marketing Strategy)

```markdown

### Who this is for
- B2B SaaS companies doing $1M-$10M ARR
- Marketing team of 1-3 people (overwhelmed, no strategy)

- Pre-revenue startups (not enough data to strategize around)

You get a 90-day marketing plan you can actually execute with your small team, plus a monthly scorecard so you know if it's working.

## Packages / Tiers

### Marketing Audit ($2,500 one-time)
- What's included: 2-week deep dive into current marketing, competitive landscape, channel analysis, customer interview synthesis
- Price: $2,500
- Timeline: 2 weeks
- Best for: Companies who aren't sure what's broken yet

### 90-Day Strategy + Monthly Advisory ($5,000/mo)
- What's included: Full strategy build + weekly 30-min calls + async Slack support + monthly scorecard review
- Price: $5,000/mo (3-month minimum)
- Timeline: Ongoing
- Best for: Companies ready to execute but need a strategic brain on retainer

## Default Recommendation

Start with the audit. It pays for itself because 80% of clients convert to the monthly retainer once they see the gaps.

| # | Objection | Response |
|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | "We can't afford $5K/mo" | "What's your current monthly ad spend? Most clients find we redirect wasted spend to cover the retainer." |
```

## Pattern 2: Small Agency (Web Design, 4 people)

### 01-Offers.md (excerpt)

```markdown
## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Who this is for
- Professional services firms (law, accounting, consulting) with 5-50 employees
- Current website is 3+ years old or was "built by a nephew"
- Revenue $500K-$5M — big enough to care about their web presence, small enough to not have in-house
- Care about looking credible to referral partners and prospective clients

### Who this is NOT for
- E-commerce (we don't do online stores)
- Startups that need a $500 template site (we're not the cheapest)
- Companies that want to redesign every 6 months (we build to last)

## Packages / Tiers

### Foundation Site ($8,000-$12,000)
- What's included: 5-8 page website, mobile responsive, basic SEO setup, 2 rounds of revisions, 1 month post-launch support
- Price: $8,000-$12,000 depending on page count
- Timeline: 4-6 weeks
- Best for: Firms that need a solid, professional site

### Growth Site ($15,000-$25,000)
- What's included: Everything in Foundation + content strategy, blog setup, lead capture forms, analytics dashboard, ongoing monthly maintenance
- Price: $15,000-$25,000 + $500/mo maintenance
- Timeline: 6-8 weeks
- Best for: Firms actively trying to generate leads from their website

## Default Recommendation

Foundation Site for most firms. Growth Site only if they have someone internally who will actually write content.
```

### 03-Delivery.md (excerpt)

```markdown
### Website Design — Foundation Site

**Definition of done**: Site is live, mobile responsive, loads in under 3 seconds, all pages reviewed and approved by client, analytics installed, basic SEO meta tags in place, client has login credentials.

**Checklist**:
- [ ] Discovery call completed + notes documented
- [ ] Sitemap approved by client
- [ ] Wireframes approved by client
- [ ] Design mockups (homepage + 1 interior page) approved
- [ ] Development complete — all pages built
- [ ] Content populated (client-provided or copywriter)
- [ ] Mobile/tablet testing passed
- [ ] Speed test: under 3 seconds on PageSpeed Insights
- [ ] SEO basics: title tags, meta descriptions, alt text
- [ ] Forms tested and connected to client's email
- [ ] Client walkthrough call completed
- [ ] Launch checklist: DNS, SSL, redirects, analytics
- [ ] Post-launch QA on live site
- [ ] Handoff: credentials, documentation, 1-month support starts

**Common pitfalls**:
- Client doesn't provide content on time (mitigation: set hard content deadline in contract, offer copywriting add-on)
- Scope creep on revisions (mitigation: contract states 2 rounds; additional rounds billed hourly)
- Going live on a Friday (mitigation: always launch Tuesday-Wednesday)
```

## Key Patterns Across All Examples

Notice what's consistent:

1. **ICPs include disqualifiers** — knowing who to say no to is as valuable as knowing who to target
2. **Pricing exists even when rough** — ranges beat blanks
3. **Next actions are single sentences** — not paragraphs, not vague
4. **Definitions of done are specific** — "Site loads in under 3 seconds" beats "site is fast"
5. **Proof is scrappy** — informal testimonials and screenshots count
6. **Ops is short** — only tools and tasks that matter make the list

## Other Common Shapes (Quick Sketches)

When you encounter these business types and have no user data yet, here's what the skill should expect:

- **Property portfolio / STR / real estate**: `01-Offers.md` becomes asset roster + pricing strategy + channel mix; `02-Pipeline.md` becomes property roster + bookings + acquisitions; `03-Delivery.md` becomes per-property SOPs (turnover checklist, guest comms, maintenance triage); `05-Ops.md` references PMS (Hospitable/Guesty/Hostfully) automations and where their reports land.
- **E-commerce**: `01-Offers.md` becomes product catalog + bundle strategy; `02-Pipeline.md` becomes inventory pipeline + new SKU launches; `03-Delivery.md` becomes order fulfillment SOPs + return handling; `05-Ops.md` references Shopify/Stripe/3PL.
- **Content creator / educator**: `01-Offers.md` becomes products (cohort, course, membership, sponsorships); `02-Pipeline.md` becomes content pipeline + sponsor pipeline; `03-Delivery.md` becomes content production SOPs; `05-Ops.md` references publishing tools and analytics.
- **SaaS startup**: `01-Offers.md` becomes pricing tiers + ICP; `02-Pipeline.md` becomes funnel by stage + activation cohorts; `03-Delivery.md` becomes onboarding + customer success motions; `05-Ops.md` references stack (auth, infra, analytics).

These are sketches, not templates. Always confirm the shape with the user before generating.
